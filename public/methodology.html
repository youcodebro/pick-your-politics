How PYP Scoring Works

PYP scoring maps each answer to party-affinity weights and issue scores, then displays a percentage-based political profile instead of assigning one fixed label.

Question Structure

The full questionnaire contains 64 questions across 8 topic modules. Each module contains 8 questions. Daily Dose mode asks 5 questions at a time so users can build a profile gradually.

Party Mix

Each answer can add weight to one or more political alignments: Democrat, Republican, Libertarian, Green, and Independent. The result is normalized into a visible party mix so users can see relative alignment rather than a binary result.

Issue Scores

PYP also tracks issue categories from negative to positive ranges so users can compare their views across specific topics. The issue score is separate from the party mix because a user may align with different coalitions on different subjects.

Methodology Influences

PYP is not affiliated with these organizations, but its neutral questionnaire design and presentation are influenced by public political research traditions and measurement approaches.

  • Pew Research Center political typology
  • American National Election Studies
  • Voteview and DW-NOMINATE

Limits

PYP results should be read as a self-assessment, not as a prediction of voting behavior. Political beliefs are contextual, and users may change their views over time.
